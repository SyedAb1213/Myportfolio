City

Rahim Yar Khan is one of the prominent cities in the Punjab province of Pakistan. It holds historical significance and is known for its cultural diversity and vibrant community. Located near the border of Sindh and Punjab, it acts as a gateway between different regions of the country. The city is well-known for its agricultural economy, producing crops like cotton, wheat, and sugarcane. It is also home to lush green fields and date palm orchards, making it one of the largest producers of dates in Pakistan. Rahim Yar Khan has a mix of urban development and traditional values. It features historical landmarks like Bhutta Wahan, believed to be the birthplace of the legendary general Mohammad Bin Qasim. The city is also near Derawar Fort, a majestic and historic fort located in the Cholistan Desert, attracting tourists and history enthusiasts. Its central location, friendly people, and growing infrastructure make Rahim Yar Khan a unique and thriving city in Pakistan.

No#01 is Islamgarh Fort

Histry of Islamgarh Fort:

Construction Built in 1665 by Raja Rawal Siri Bheem Singh, a Hindu Rajput king of Jaisalmer, the fort was originally called Bheem War Fort. The fort was built using materials from Jaisalmer, India, and locally made bricks. Capture and renaming In 1780, Muslim ruler Ikhtiar Khan Sund-i-Elahi captured the fort and renamed it Islamgarh Fort. He also built a mosque inside the fort. Ownership After Khan's defeat, the fort fell into the hands of Nawab Muhammad Mubarak Khan II. Location Islamgarh Fort is located in the Khanpur Tehsil of Rahim Yar Khan District, Punjab Province, close to the Indian border. Features The fort is square in plan, with high and sloping walls strengthened by bastions. The fort is made of burnt bricks that are 19.05 x 14 x 2.5 cm in size. Status Islamgarh Fort is a cultural and historical landmark. However, it is privately owned and not protected.

No#02 is Bhong Mosque

Bhong Mosque history

The Bhong Mosque in Rahim Yar Khan, Pakistan, was designed and built over nearly 50 years, beginning in 1932 and completed in 1982: The landlord: The late Rais Ghazi Mohammad, the landlord of a large estate, started construction of the mosque as the centerpiece of his palace compound. The design: The mosque's builders drew inspiration from a variety of styles, including Lahore, Iran, Spain, Turkey, and Western colonial elements from the 1940s. The award: The mosque won the 1986 Aga Khan Award for Architecture. The impact: The mosque's construction helped to revitalize local crafts and served as a center for the formation of the village core. The architecture: The mosque is a complex structure with three domes, many small minarets, a main prayer hall, a garden, a women's prayer hall, a porch, a courtyard, a library, a school hall, and ulama's quarters. The decoration: The mosque is known for its colorful and elegant decoration. The role: The mosque serves as a center of learning, with students from Turkey, Afghanistan, and Iran.
